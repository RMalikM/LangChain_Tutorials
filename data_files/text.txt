The story of human history begins approximately 300,000 years ago with the emergence of Homo sapiens in Africa.

Early humans lived as hunter-gatherers, relying on nature for food and shelter.

Around 10,000 BCE, the Agricultural Revolution marked a major turning point as humans began farming and domesticating animals.

Permanent settlements formed, leading to the rise of early civilizations such as Mesopotamia, Ancient Egypt, and the Indus Valley.

Writing systems emerged around 3,000 BCE, allowing the recording of laws, trade, and culture.

Classical civilizations like Greece, Rome, and China flourished, contributing to philosophy, science, and governance.

The Middle Ages (5th to 15th century) saw the rise of kingdoms, the spread of religions, and feudal systems.

The Renaissance (14th to 17th century) brought renewed interest in art, science, and humanism in Europe.

The Age of Exploration expanded global trade and colonization, driven by European powers.

The Industrial Revolution (18th to 19th century) transformed economies with machines, factories, and urbanization.

The 20th century witnessed two World Wars, decolonization, and major technological advancements.

The Digital Age began in the late 20th century, revolutionizing communication, information, and daily life.

Today, humanity faces challenges such as climate change, inequality, and the ethical use of AI, while continuing to make scientific and cultural progress.
